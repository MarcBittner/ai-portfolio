# Gadget confessions, a Q&A with myself

Q: Why do I keep buying kitchen gadgets I never use?
A: Because the reviews are persuasive and I am an optimist about my
future habits. The juicer is exhibit A. It has juiced four times in two
years, which works out to roughly a small hardcover per juice.

Q: What is the worst offender?
A: The bread maker. It makes one shape of loaf — a sort of melancholy
cube — and the paddle bakes itself into the bottom of every loaf like a
prize in a cereal box.

Q: Any gadget I actually love?
A: The thermometer for proofing yeast, oddly. Three dollars. Used twice
a week. There is a lesson there about price and joy that I decline to
learn at checkout.

Q: Will the balcony garden be different?
A: Yes, because dirt has no subscription fee.
