# Research notes: Gullsworth book four

Working title: *A Tide of Small Lies*. Setting research from the coast
trip in May.

## Tides and alibis

The harbor at my reference town runs a 3.4 meter tidal range on spring
tides. If the body is found at the boathouse at low water, the window
for the rowboat to have crossed is barely forty minutes. This is
excellent news for the plot and bad news for Gerald, my suspect.

## Lighthouse logistics

The keeper's log tradition: weather, ships sighted, oil consumed,
visitors. A missing page would be noticed by the district inspector at
the monthly check — better to have the page altered, not removed.
Fountain pen ink can be dated by its iron-gall content. My murderer
does not know this. My detective does.

## Local color

- The bakery sells out of saffron buns by 9 a.m. on Saturdays
- Fishermen mend nets on the seawall and hear everything
- Everyone claims not to gossip, which is how you know they all do
