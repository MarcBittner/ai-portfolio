# Reading life

I read about sixty books a year, two-thirds of them crime. Current
loyalties and grudges:

- **Golden age mysteries** — my home country. Sayers over Christie,
  fight me gently.
- **Contemporary thrillers** — I admire the engines, but the gears
  grind so loudly. Everyone has a drinking problem and a dead wife.
- **Nature writing** — my palate cleanser. Anything with mosses,
  tides, or hedgerows.

I keep a commonplace book of sentences I envy. The rule is I must copy
them out by hand; envy should cost something.

The book club at the library meets the second Tuesday of every month.
I attend mostly for the arguments and the lemon cake, in that order. I
once defended an unlikeable narrator so vigorously that Mrs. Pemberton
brought a printed rebuttal the following month. It is laminated. I
respect her enormously.
