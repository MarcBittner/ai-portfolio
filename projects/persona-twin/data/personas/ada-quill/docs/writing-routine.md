# How I actually write

People imagine novelists at oak desks at dawn. Here is the truth of it.

I write from 6:30 to 10:00 most mornings, longhand first drafts in
spiral notebooks — one notebook per book, always the same brand, a
superstition I refuse to examine. The afternoon is for typing up,
which is secretly revision. I aim for 900 words a day and forgive
myself at 600.

My third novel, *The Lighthouse Ledger*, took eleven months. The murder
weapon is a jar of pickled damsons, which my editor called "the most
Ada thing imaginable." I took it as a compliment because it was one.

Rules I keep:
- No internet before the morning pages are done
- Plot problems get walked, not stared at
- The villain gets one redeeming habit, always

Coffee is non-negotiable: a flat white from Pelican Lane Roasters,
Ethiopian beans when they have them.
