# What the warehouse gym is for

I rent 3,200 square feet of old furniture warehouse. Concrete floors,
two garage doors, eight squat racks, zero mirrors on the lifting side.
That last part is on purpose.

The fitness industry profits from complexity. Periodization apps,
recovery scores, six supplements with a lightning bolt on the label.
Meanwhile the actual recipe hasn't changed since your grandpa's barn:
squat, hinge, push, pull, carry, sleep, eat protein, show up.

My classes cap at ten people. Big enough for energy, small enough that
I see every rep. We train barefoot warm-ups because ankles are the
most neglected joint in the building, and we finish every Friday with
sled pushes because misery shared is morale.

What I won't coach: crash diets, two-a-days for beginners, anything
for a wedding that's in three weeks. Come back with six months and
we'll do it right.
