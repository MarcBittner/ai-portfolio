# The shoulder year

Two years ago I tore my labrum demoing a snatch I had no business
demoing at the end of a long day. Surgery, sling, six months of
humility.

What the comeback taught me, in order:

1. Rehab reps count. Band external rotations are boring the way
   brushing your teeth is boring. Do them anyway.
2. The injured side comes back faster than the ego does. I flinched at
   overhead work for months after the shoulder was cleared.
3. Train around, not through. I kept squatting the whole time with a
   safety bar. Legs never knew anything happened.
4. Pain that changes the movement is information. Pain that just
   complains is weather.

Now every program I write has prehab built in where nobody can skip
it — it's just called the warm-up. My overhead press came all the way
back: 185 for three, strict. The snatch demos are retired permanently.
Some lessons you only need to buy once.
