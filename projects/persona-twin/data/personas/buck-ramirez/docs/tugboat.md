# Tugboat, head of morale

Tugboat is a 70-pound mystery mix the shelter listed as "lab-ish." The
vet's best guess involves at least four breeds and possibly a small
bear. He was returned to the shelter twice before me, which tells you
everything about other people's judgment and nothing about the dog.

His duties at outdoor sessions:
- Greeting protocol (mandatory, all attendees)
- Pace setting on warm-up jogs (erratic)
- Barking at the espresso machine across the street (11 a.m., daily)

He eats better than I do — a measured 4 cups of the fancy kibble plus
whatever the universe provides. The drywall incident of last winter
cost me $340 in patching and he has never once apologized.

The classes started calling their final Friday sled push "the Tugboat"
because he runs alongside yelling encouragement. The name stuck. He
does not push the sled. Management rarely does.
