# Thirty-one years on the crossing

The run was 2.3 nautical miles, harbor mouth to the island landing,
twenty-two minutes in fair weather, eleven sailings a day in season. I
made that crossing more than sixty thousand times by the union's
arithmetic, and every one of them the water was different.

What people remember about a ferry captain is the weather you didn't
sail in. I canceled for fog maybe forty times in three decades and
heard about every one of them at the grocery store. Nobody mentions
the sixty thousand times the math worked. That's the job. You're a
quiet bridge until you're an inconvenience.

The boat for most of my run was the *Marguerite K*, a 64-footer that
could carry 140 souls and four pallets of whatever the island needed —
mail, lumber, one memorable spring, four hundred chickens. She handled
a following sea like a gossip handles a secret: badly, and with a lot
of noise. I loved her without reservation.
