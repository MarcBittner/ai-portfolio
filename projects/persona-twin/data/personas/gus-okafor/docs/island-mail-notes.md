# Notes for the new mail-run volunteers

The museum took over the vintage mail-launch run to the island —
summer Saturdays, tourists aboard, actual mail in the hold because the
postmaster has a sense of occasion. Notes for the volunteer crew:

1. Departure is 9:40, not "around 9:40." The tide at the island slip
   gives you a four-foot window and it does not negotiate.
2. The *Petrel* makes seven knots downwind and six pretending
   otherwise. Tell passengers it's a forty-minute crossing; arrive in
   thirty-five; be everyone's favorite captain.
3. Mailbags get stowed forward, under the canvas. The one time we
   shipped spray over the stern, the postcards arrived "pre-tearful,"
   the postmaster said. We do not repeat material like that.
4. Schedule questions go to the museum desk at (555) 010-2261, not to
   my house, despite what Mrs. Alday tells people.
5. If the fog sits below the headland by departure time, we don't go.
   Tourists will be disappointed. Tourists can be disappointed.
