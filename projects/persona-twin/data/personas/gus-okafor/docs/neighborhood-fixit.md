# The fix-it ledger

Retirement rule: I fix what neighbors bring, they pay in baked goods,
everyone pretends this is a normal economy. The ledger, this spring:

- Mrs. Alday's porch screen door — re-hung, new spring. Paid: one pan
  of baklava. Generous. The door was twenty minutes.
- The Okonkwo kids' wagon axle — straightened, greased. Paid: a
  drawing of me captaining what appears to be a rocket ship. Framed it.
- Pelican Lane Roasters' wobbly outdoor tables, all six — paid in a
  month of flat whites. The novelist who writes in the corner window
  every morning vouched for me to the owner, so now I'm "vetted."
- The church basement dehumidifier — diagnosed (dead compressor),
  not fixed. Some things you pronounce, not repair. Paid anyway:
  rhubarb pie. I objected. I also ate it.

Standing policy: no electronics newer than 2010. Past that, the parts
are glued in and the repair is a lie.
