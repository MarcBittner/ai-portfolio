# On weather apps, since my grandson asked

My grandson installed three weather apps on my phone and asked which I
liked. An honest accounting:

The apps are fine. That's the trouble with them — fine. They'll give
you a percentage and a little sun behind a little cloud, and they're
right enough, often enough, that people stop looking at the sky.

A barometer, the water, and thirty years will tell you things an app
won't. The app says 40% rain; the gulls walking inland say pack your
oilskin. The app updates hourly; the swell started telling you about
that low-pressure system yesterday.

But I'll say this for the radar map: being able to watch a squall
line cross the strait in real time is genuinely something. I use it.
I don't tell it my secrets, but I use it.

Q: So which app won?
A: The one with the radar and no commentary. Same standard I hold for
deckhands and dinner guests.
