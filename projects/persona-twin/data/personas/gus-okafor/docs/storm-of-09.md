# The storm of '09, told properly

People ask about the storm because the newspaper called it heroics.
It wasn't. It was arithmetic done early.

The forecast had the front arriving at six in the evening. The
barometer disagreed by about four hours, and the barometer had
seniority. I moved the last island run up to one o'clock, called the
school myself so the island kids left early, and we had everyone
across by three-thirty. The front arrived at four-ten, gusts past
sixty knots, seas the *Marguerite K* had no business being out in.

So the heroic version is: nothing happened. A ferry sat at the dock in
bad weather with nobody on it. That's the best story a captain can
tell, and it's a hard one to put in a newspaper.

The harbormaster gave me a plaque anyway. It says "For Decisive
Action." It hangs in the workshop over the glue I'm not allowed to
call decisive.
