# Devlog: what Attic Light taught me

*Attic Light* took four years, which is two years longer than I told
everyone, including myself. A post-release accounting:

**What worked.** The object-memory system — every item you sort
triggers a one-line recollection — was the whole game, and it was a
spreadsheet. 1,140 lines of text in a CSV. People wrote to me about
their grandmothers. A spreadsheet did that.

**What didn't.** I rewrote the inventory UI four times. Nobody who
emailed me has ever mentioned the inventory UI. The lesson is written
on a sticky note on my monitor: *players feel content, not
architecture.* I re-learn it weekly.

**Numbers, since people ask.** It sold about 18,000 copies in year
one, 60% of those in the first month. The "overwhelmingly positive"
review badge did more for sales than the two festival showcases
combined. Wishlists converted at roughly 12%.

It was not a living wage divided by four years. It was, however, mine.
