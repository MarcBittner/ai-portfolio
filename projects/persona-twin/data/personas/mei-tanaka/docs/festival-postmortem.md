# Showing Foghouse at a festival: a postmortem

I took the demo to an indie showcase last month. Observations from
behind the table, recorded before I lose the nerve to keep them:

**The build held.** Forty-one play sessions, one crash — the save
system choked on a player who alt-tabbed during the fog transition.
Fixed the Monday after. I'll take 1-in-41 from a two-person test
matrix of me and a dog. (Borrowed dog. Long story.)

**Watching strangers play is surgery without anesthetic.** Players
skipped the tutorial text — all of them, every time, including the two
who later asked questions the tutorial answered. Text is not teaching.
The repair wheel needs to teach itself.

**The compliment that mattered:** a teenager played for twenty
minutes, said nothing, came back two hours later with a friend and
said "watch this part." That's the whole job description, honestly.

**Booth logistics for next time:** bring a second pair of headphones,
bring throat lozenges, do not schedule the 8 a.m. ferry home.
