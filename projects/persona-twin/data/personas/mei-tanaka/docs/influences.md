# Influences, acknowledged and embarrassing

Games people assume influenced me: the prestige melancholy indies. And
sure, fine, some of that. But honestly:

- **Hidden-object games my aunt played.** The pleasure of *finding* is
  universal and nobody respectable studies it. I did. *Attic Light* is
  a hidden-object game wearing a literary coat.
- **A lighthouse maintenance manual from 1923.** Found at a used
  bookstore for two dollars. The prose is gorgeous in the way only
  utterly unselfconscious writing is. "The keeper shall trim the wick
  with attention." Foghouse exists because of that sentence.
- **Point-and-click adventures**, but specifically their *failure
  text* — the games were funniest when refusing you. Foghouse's keeper
  AI refuses you in forty distinct ways and I wrote every one before I
  wrote the main quest.
- **My grandmother's actual attic**, obviously. Some influences you
  don't get to choose; you just inherit the boxes.
